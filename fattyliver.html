The Fatty Liver Index runs 0–100: < 30 makes hepatic steatosis unlikely, ≥ 60 makes it likely. Values marked study average use the mean of the Buchinger Wilhelmi study population when a measurement is unknown.

FLI trajectory over the fast

Projected daily FLI from baseline to the end of the selected fasting duration. Threshold lines mark the steatosis-unlikely (< 30) and steatosis-likely (≥ 60) cut-offs.

For informational purposes only. This estimator was built from paired baseline and end-of-fast data of more than 2,800 patients across the Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting cohorts. The Fatty Liver Index (Bedogni 2006) is computed from triglycerides, body-mass index, GGT and waist circumference; its change during fasting is modelled from the baseline index and fasting duration, predicting the end-of-fast index with a mean accuracy of 85% (R² = 0.85). The Fatty Liver Index is a screening score, not a diagnosis. Always consult a qualified medical professional before undertaking a prolonged fast.

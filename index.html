Chapter 01 · The Foundation

The World's Largest
Fasting Study

Published in PLOS ONE in 2019, the largest prospective fasting study ever conducted enrolled 1,422 subjects undergoing 4–21 days of medically supervised fasting at Buchinger Wilhelmi. Drawing on a decade of clinical records, it established the safety, effectiveness, and well-being outcomes of therapeutic fasting at an unprecedented scale.

Of 404 patients with pre-existing health complaints, 341 — 84% — reported improvement. Conditions included arthritis, type 2 diabetes, fatty liver, hypertension, and high cholesterol.

Chapter 02 · Weight

Real Weight Loss,
Real Results

Across 1,422 patients, weight fell consistently and substantially — up to −11.1 kg in men over 20 fasting days. The loss is predominantly fat mass, not muscle.

Among hypertensive patients on antihypertensive medication at the start of fasting: 23.6% stopped medication entirely by the end of the fasting period. A further 43.5% reduced their dosage. Only 2.9% required an increase. These changes were made under medical supervision as clinical parameters improved.

Chapter 04 · Fat Elimination

The Right Fat,
Eliminated

Fasting preferentially targets visceral fat — the metabolically harmful fat surrounding abdominal organs. An MRI study confirmed a 20.8% reduction in visceral fat, with liver fat dropping from 2.6% to 1.4%. Among 697 patients with elevated fatty liver index, almost half shifted to a lower risk category after fasting. Each additional fasting day increased the probability of improvement by 40%.

−20.8% Visceral fat reduction (MRI-confirmed, EJCI 2019)
40% Increased probability of improving fatty liver per fasting day
Chapter 05 · Blood Sugar

Blood Sugar Normalised —
Type 2 Diabetes Reversed

Fasting blood glucose consistently decreases to low-normal range during Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting, reflecting the shift from glucose to fat and ketone metabolism — a benefit seen across all 1,422 patients in the foundation study regardless of diabetic status.

A 2025 case report (Frontiers) documented a 57-year-old with uncontrolled type 2 diabetes who, through repeated long-term fasting, achieved improved HbA1c, restored insulin sensitivity, and markedly reduced dependence on hypoglycaemic drugs — a clinically documented reversal of T2DM.

Chapter 06 · Inflammation

Long COVID —
New Hope

A 2023 case series and literature review examined the effects of long-term fasting on five patients suffering from long COVID — a heterogeneous post-infectious syndrome with limited therapeutic options. All patients showed improvements in overall perceived health. Specific improvements included: fatigue, breathlessness, muscle and joint pain, and — notably — cognitive impairment, and disorders of smell and taste.

The biological mechanisms proposed include fasting's anti-inflammatory effects, autophagy induction, immune system recalibration, and gut microbiome remodelling. The authors called for formal randomised clinical trials.

Chapter 07 · Wellbeing

Wellbeing and Mental
Health Improved

Fasting does not cause distress — it relieves it. In the 1,422-patient study, 93.2% of participants reported improvements in physical and emotional wellbeing during fasting, and the absence of hunger. A 2025 GENESIS sub-study showed that fasting shifts the nervous system into a calmer, more restorative state — better sleep, better mood, lower stress. This happens because fasting activates the parasympathetic ("rest and digest") branch of the nervous system, measurably improving heart rate variability.

93.2% Reported improved wellbeing during fasting

PLOS ONE 2019 · GENESIS autonomic sub-study, Int J Obesity 2025

Better Sleep, Better Mood,
Less Stress

The GENESIS study used heart rate variability (HRV) — a precise measure of nervous system balance — to show that 10 days of fasting causes a significant shift toward the parasympathetic nervous system. In simple terms: the body moves from a stressed, alert state into a calm, regenerative one. Patients consistently report improved sleep quality, reduced anxiety, and greater emotional resilience during and after fasting.

The GENESIS cardiac MRI sub-study also documented substantial reductions in pericardial and hepatic fat — hidden fat depots linked to heart and metabolic risk that standard body weight measurements fail to detect.

Cardiac MRI · GENESIS Study
Chapter 08 · Muscle & Energy

Muscle Preserved.
Mitochondria Boosted.

Fasting does not destroy muscle — this fear is not supported by the evidence. In the GENESIS study (12 days, 250 kcal/day): body weight fell by 7.4% while skeletal muscle volume fell by just 5.4% — a substantially better ratio than seen with equivalent calorie-restricted diets. Muscle strength was maintained and improved by up to 33% in some participants. At the cellular level, fasting activates mitochondrial biogenesis — boosting the energy-producing capacity of muscle cells.

−5.4% Muscle volume lost vs −7.4% body weight
+33% Muscle strength gain in best-responding participants
Chapter 09 · Longevity

Fasting Activates the
Biology of Longevity

Fasting triggers the body's own anti-ageing programme: activating autophagy (cellular self-cleaning), AMPK, SIRT1, and inhibiting mTOR — the same pathways that extend lifespan in all organisms studied. No drug replicates this breadth of effect.

The ultimate proof: a 92-year-old who fasted 21 days annually for 45 consecutive years under Buchinger Wilhelmi supervision (JICM 2024). Epigenetic analysis placed his biological age 5.9 years below his chronological age. No frailty. Full mobility. Excellent cognition. His clinical parameters returned reliably to baseline after every refeeding cycle.

−5.9 years Biological age advantage at 92 · 45 years of annual fasting

Wilhelmi de Toledo, Grundler, Mesnage — JICM 2024

Research History

A Century of
Fasting Science

1919

Otto Buchinger Pioneers Therapeutic Fasting

Recovering from rheumatoid arthritis through a supervised fast, Otto Buchinger develops therapeutic fasting as a systematic clinical practice and establishes the first fasting clinic in Germany.

1953

Clinic Opens on Lake Constance

Buchinger Wilhelmi opens at Bodensee, integrating medical supervision, structured movement, nutritional refeeding, and psycho-spiritual support into a programme still running today.

2002

Expert Consensus Guidelines

Fasting therapy guidelines co-authored by Buchinger Wilhelmi clinicians are published — the first evidence-based framework for medically supervised fasting, updated in 2013.

2019

Landmark 1,422-Patient PLOS ONE Study

The world's largest prospective fasting study is published — 1,422 patients, a decade of records — becoming the most-cited evidence base for therapeutic fasting worldwide.

2021

GENESIS Multi-System Trial Launches

The most comprehensive clinical fasting trial to date begins, integrating metabolomics, metagenomics, cardiac MRI, HRV analysis, and 4-month follow-up across 100 participants.

2024–25

Virome, Longevity & Refeeding Research

New frontiers: the gut virome in fasting; 45-year repeated fasting case; biological age clocks; refeeding optimisation. The evidence base continues to grow — openly published and openly accessible.

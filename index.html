Our Scientific Studies

Below is a selection of our recent publications. Each card links to a detailed article in our research library.

Time-restricted eating may be easier to sustain than calorie counting

After a 12-week intervention, an 8-hour eating window helped participants maintain weight for up to 6 months, with many reporting better sleep and digestion compared with calorie tracking.

Learn more

Calorie restriction reduces anxiety by calming brain inflammation in mice

In obese mice, calorie restriction reduced anxiety-like behaviour and improved insulin sensitivity, alongside shifts in gut microbes that increased short-chain fatty acids linked to brain and metabolic health.

Learn more

Hypoxic training amplifies the lipid-lowering effects of calorie restriction

In men with obesity and high cholesterol, adding intermittent hypoxic training to calorie restriction for 4 weeks led to larger drops in LDL cholesterol and triglycerides than the same programme under normal oxygen.

Chapter 01 · Metabolism

The Metabolic Switch

Within 24 to 36 hours of fasting, the body exhausts its hepatic glycogen stores and shifts its primary energy substrate from glucose to fatty acids and ketone bodies. This "metabolic switch" is a deeply conserved evolutionary programme and the cornerstone of fasting physiology.

The GENESIS study (Grundler, Mesnage et al., Frontiers in Nutrition, 2022) was designed to characterise the multi-system adaptations induced by 10-day fasting at Buchinger Wilhelmi, tracking blood, urine, and tissue markers at baseline, post-fasting, and 1- and 4-month follow-up visits.

Ketosis in 1,610 Subjects

A 2024 analysis of 1,610 patients undergoing Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting documented the kinetics and safety of fasting-induced ketosis at scale. Beta-hydroxybutyrate (BHB) rose markedly across all patients, reaching peak levels between days 3 and 7 — confirming that deep, consistent ketosis is safely achievable across diverse patient populations including those with metabolic comorbidities.

1,610 Patients · Nutrients 2024

Long-term fasting-induced ketosis: metabolic regulation and safety — Grundler, Mesnage, Ruppert, Kouretas, Wilhelmi de Toledo

Erythrocyte Membrane Remodelling

A 2025 study in the European Journal of Clinical Investigation found that 10 days of fasting substantially remodels the fatty acid composition of red blood cell membranes. Saturated and monounsaturated fatty acids declined; omega-3 polyunsaturated fatty acids increased — a favourable shift with direct implications for cardiovascular membrane biology.

These changes in membrane fluidity represent a novel cellular-level biomarker of prolonged fasting, opening new windows into how fasting reshapes cell physiology beyond standard blood panels.

Foundation Study · 2019

1,422

The world's largest prospective fasting study — documenting the safety, health improvements, and well-being of patients across 4 to 21 days of Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting.

Safety, first and always

Across 1,422 subjects spanning a decade of clinical records, fasting was overwhelmingly safe. Adverse events were rare and minor. The study documented improvements in body weight, blood pressure, fasting glucose, lipid profiles, and quality of life across the full cohort — establishing Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting as a rigorously evidenced clinical intervention.

Wilhelmi de Toledo, Grundler, Bergouignan, Drinda & Michalsen — PLOS ONE, 2019.

Chapter 02 · Biomarkers

Blood Pressure &
1,610 Patients

A 2020 study in the Journal of the American Heart Association documented blood pressure changes in 1,610 patients — with and without antihypertensive medication — during Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting. Both groups showed significant, clinically meaningful reductions in systolic and diastolic blood pressure.

−17 mmHg Mean systolic BP reduction

In hypertensive patients during 10-day Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting — Grundler, Mesnage et al., JAHA 2020

Inflammation — Baseline Matters

A 2025 paper in Frontiers in Nutrition revisited the anti-inflammatory effects of prolonged fasting and identified a critical but overlooked variable: baseline CRP level.

Re-analysing the clinical dataset, Mesnage, Grundler and Wilhelmi de Toledo showed that patients with elevated baseline CRP experienced the greatest reductions during fasting — while those with already-low CRP showed minimal change. This "baseline-dependent response" reframes the anti-inflammatory evidence and has direct clinical implications for patient selection and outcome prediction.

HDL Function &
Cardiovascular Risk

A 2024 prospective study assessed HDL cholesterol efflux capacity during long-term fasting — a functional measure of how effectively HDL removes cholesterol from arterial walls, widely considered more predictive of cardiovascular risk than total HDL levels.

Fasting improved both efflux capacity and cholesterol loading capacity in erythrocyte membranes, pointing to qualitative improvements in cardiovascular protective function beyond what standard lipid panels detect.

Atherosclerosis 2024 · Prospective study

HDL cholesterol efflux capacity in long-term fasting — Grundler, Palumbo, Adorni, Zimetti, Papotti, Plonné

Chapter 03 · Microbiome

Gut Microbiota & the
Metabolic Switch

A 2019 study in the Journal of Nutritional Science was the first to directly link gut microbiota compositional changes to the metabolic switch during 10-day Buchinger fasting. As the energy substrate shifted from glucose to ketones, the intestinal microbial community reorganised — with shifts in Firmicutes-to-Bacteroidetes ratios and enrichment of anti-inflammatory taxa.

Remodelling the
Gut Virome

A December 2025 preprint on medRxiv reported the first comprehensive characterisation of the gut virome — the community of bacteriophages that regulate microbial dynamics — in the context of long-term fasting.

Using metagenomics across discovery and validation cohorts from Buchinger Wilhelmi clinical trials, the team identified consistent virome signatures post-fasting, including enrichment of phages linked to metabolically beneficial bacteria. This opens an unexplored dimension of the microbiome-fasting interface.

Gut Virome medRxiv · December 2025

Remodelling of the gut virome after long-term fasting — Falshaw N, Ducarmon QR, King A, Grundler F, Mesnage R

Intestinal Ecosystem —
A 2023 Review

A 2023 review in Trends in Microbiology, co-authored by Buchinger Wilhelmi researchers, synthesised the evidence on how caloric restriction and fasting reorganise the intestinal ecosystem.

The review positioned the gut as an active participant in fasting physiology — connecting fasting-induced microbial metabolite shifts to host immune function, gut barrier integrity, and the anti-inflammatory effects documented clinically.

Case Study · 2024

45 years

The world's longest medically documented repeated fasting history — a 92-year-old man who fasted 21 days annually for 45 years. Biological age analysis placed him 5.9 years younger than his chronological age. No frailty. Excellent cognition. Full mobility.

Fasting as a
lifelong practice

Published in the Journal of Integrative and Complementary Medicine in 2024, this extraordinary case report provides the first long-term documented evidence that repeated annual fasting cycles are compatible with exceptional longevity and function into extreme old age. Body weight and clinical parameters cycled predictably, always returning to pre-fasting baselines after refeeding.

Wilhelmi de Toledo F, Grundler F, Mesnage R — JICM, 2024.

Chapter 04 · Autonomic System

The Nervous System
in Fasting

The GENESIS study included a cardiovascular autonomic sub-study published in the International Journal of Obesity in 2025. Using heart rate variability (HRV) analysis, the team characterised autonomic nervous system (ANS) modulation during and after 10-day fasting in a subgroup of participants.

HRV is a sensitive index of the balance between sympathetic ("fight or flight") and parasympathetic ("rest and digest") nervous system activity. Disrupted autonomic balance is associated with cardiovascular disease, metabolic syndrome, and accelerated ageing.

Parasympathetic Upregulation

Fasting induced a statistically significant shift toward parasympathetic dominance — evidenced by increased high-frequency HRV components and reduced sympathetic indicators. The effect peaked around day 7 and showed partial persistence at the 1-month follow-up visit.

This autonomic recalibration may help explain the subjective improvements in mood, sleep quality, and stress resilience that Buchinger Wilhelmi patients consistently report — bridging the molecular observations with patient-reported outcomes.

Int J Obesity 2025 · GENESIS Sub-Study

Mesnage R, Holley A, Grundler F, Martinez-Tellez B, Wilhelmi de Toledo F, Croisille P

Cardiac MRI &
Ectopic Fat

The GENESIS protocol also incorporated cardiac MRI to quantify pericardial, epicardial, and hepatic fat — ectopic fat depots that predict cardiometabolic risk independently of total body weight and that standard BMI-based assessments fail to capture.

Ten days of supervised fasting produced substantial reductions in all three compartments, demonstrating that Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting achieves targeted reductions in metabolically harmful fat that conventional caloric restriction approaches typically reach only after many months.

Chapter 05 · Longevity

Hallmarks of Ageing

The Buchinger Wilhelmi scientific programme has documented that therapeutic fasting activates core hallmarks-of-ageing pathways — slowing cell growth, reducing oxidative stress, and inducing autophagy. These are the same pathways targeted by caloric restriction interventions that extend lifespan in animal models.

Fasting upregulates AMPK, inhibits mTOR signalling, and activates SIRT1 — a molecular signature associated with decelerated ageing and improved metabolic healthspan.

Biological Age &
Epigenetic Clocks

Epigenetic biological age — estimated from DNA methylation patterns — is a validated surrogate for physiological ageing rate. Initial analyses from the Buchinger Wilhelmi cohort indicate that patients engaging in repeated long-term fasting tend to display biological ages below their chronological ages.

The 2024 case report of a 92-year-old patient with 45 years of annual fasting showed a biological age 5.9 years below his chronological age — no frailty, excellent cognition, intact mobility.

−5.9 years Biological age advantage

92-year-old man, 45 consecutive years of annual 21-day fasting. Biological age below chronological age — Wilhelmi de Toledo, Grundler, Mesnage, JICM 2024

Current & Future
Research

A 2024 study launched at Buchinger Wilhelmi focuses on the refeeding phase — how the critical food reintroduction period after prolonged fasting can be optimised to consolidate and extend the metabolic gains achieved during the fast itself.

Active research also covers the musculoskeletal system, joint inflammation and chronic pain, and the longer-term follow-up of patients who practise fasting repeatedly over years — building the first longitudinal evidence base for the cumulative health effects of therapeutic fasting.

Research History

A Century of
Fasting Science

1919

Otto Buchinger Pioneers Therapeutic Fasting

Recovering from rheumatoid arthritis through a supervised fast, Otto Buchinger develops therapeutic fasting as a systematic clinical practice and establishes the first fasting clinic in Germany.

1953

Clinic Opens on Lake Constance

Buchinger Wilhelmi opens at Bodensee, integrating medical supervision, structured movement, nutritional refeeding, and psycho-spiritual support into a programme still running today.

2002

Expert Consensus Guidelines

Fasting therapy guidelines co-authored by Buchinger Wilhelmi clinicians are published — the first evidence-based framework for medically supervised fasting, updated in 2013.

2019

Landmark 1,422-Patient PLOS ONE Study

The world's largest prospective fasting study is published — 1,422 patients, a decade of records — becoming the most-cited evidence base for therapeutic fasting worldwide.

2021

GENESIS Multi-System Trial Launches

The most comprehensive clinical fasting trial to date begins, integrating metabolomics, metagenomics, cardiac MRI, HRV analysis, and 4-month follow-up across 100 participants.

2024–25

Virome, Longevity & Refeeding Research

New frontiers: the gut virome in fasting; 45-year repeated fasting case; biological age clocks; refeeding optimisation. The evidence base continues to grow — openly published and openly accessible.

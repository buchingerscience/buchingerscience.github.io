Project 01 · Flagship Clinical Study

FastForward Study

Confirms long-term efficacy of fasting and acts as innovation test ground

FastForward is our flagship clinical study aimed at improving post-fasting outcomes. We have collected data on more than 300 individuals, generating one of the most comprehensive fasting datasets ever assembled:

  • 840 venous blood samples including during food reintroduction
  • 720 dry blood samples to understand persistence of fasting effects at home
  • 3,920 days of continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) data during food reintroduction
  • 3,920 days of food records during food reintroduction
  • 500 stool samples
  • 120 PBMC samples
  • 350 days of continuous ketone monitoring data
  • 1,120 questionnaires

Key objectives include comparing ketogenic and non-ketogenic food reintroduction; evaluating fasting's impact on glucose responses, immune function, autophagy, and biological age; establishing a structured follow-up programme for patients using wearable devices and at-home testing tools; and creating a long-term observational study model integrated into clinic operations.

Project 02 · Women's Health

Women's Health Study

Generates evidence across menstrual and menopausal phases

We launched a Women's Health Survey to explore how fasting affects the menstrual cycle, menopausal symptoms, and overall well-being across different life stages. The questionnaire captures data before fasting, immediately afterward, and at 1- and 3-month follow-ups. It covers menstrual regularity, menopause-related symptoms, hormonal treatments, fertility history, and emotional and physical health.

Project 03 · Multi-System Trial

GENESIS – Cardiac & Liver Health

Continues documenting changes in body composition and function

The GENESIS study continues. Using quantitative MRI, we studied 32 participants of a 12-day fasting intervention with follow-up at 1 and 4 months to understand changes in liver, subcutaneous, and visceral fat volumes. Our partners at the University of Lyon are also studying how cardiac parameters change during fasting at an unprecedented level of detail.

Project 04 · Gut Health

CoFFIe – Dietary Fibres × Fasting

Develops a new fasting programme optimised for gut health

RCT €1M Grant LUMC · TNO

We have initiated the CoFFIe trial, a randomized controlled study designed to investigate whether supplementing long-term fasting with dietary fibre can enhance health outcomes through gut microbiome modulation. Seventy-five participants will undergo a 10 ± 3 day Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting programme. Fifty will receive maize-derived resistant starch type IV (fibre), while twenty-five will receive a placebo.

Multi-omics analyses — metagenomics, metabolomics, and transcriptomics — and clinical markers will be used to track changes in metabolism. The study is conducted in partnership with Leiden University Medical Center (LUMC) and TNO, as part of a successful grant application that secured €1 million in funding, with the clinic as a key partner.

Project 05 · Physical Activity

FASTEX – Fasting Meets Fitness

Creates a reproducible movement protocol to integrate into fasting care

RCT University of Almería

We are collaborating with the team of Dr. Borja Martinez-Tellez at the University of Almería on a randomized controlled trial exploring the synergy between fasting, physical activity, and sleep quality in older adults. Participants are divided into three groups:

  • Standard Buchinger Wilhelmi programme
  • Home-based familiarisation programme followed by the standard Buchinger Wilhelmi programme
  • Home-based familiarisation programme with a structured resistance training programme during the clinic stay, developed with the University of Almería team

Project 06 · Mechanistic Research

Milan Collaboration – UNION

Adds mechanistic depth to our scientific narrative on fasting and regeneration

European Project

We collaborate with the team of Massimiliano Ruscica as part of the UNION project, which brings together new collaborations to deepen our mechanistic understanding of fasting. This is a large European project focused on extracellular vesicles and autophagy where Buchinger Wilhelmi appears as an industrial partner.

Project 07 · Prehabilitation

Prehabilitation Study – Charité Berlin

Strategic move into hospital care pathways with fasting before surgery

RCT Charité Universitätsmedizin Berlin

In cooperation with Charité – Universitätsmedizin Berlin, a randomized clinical trial on preoperative metabolic optimization in hip arthroplasty is currently underway. The study investigates whether a structured preoperative fasting intervention, delivered with the fasting box, can influence the gut microbiome, immunological markers, and postoperative complications.

The intervention is implemented using fasting boxes and supported by a dedicated app created by our scientific department, as well as continuous glucose monitoring. Collected data include ketone bodies, anthropometric measures, blood pressure, and glucose values. Immune status analyses and shotgun metagenomic sequencing of the gut microbiome are performed at defined time points before and after surgery.

Science

A time-proven practice rooted in science and wisdom

A century of medically supervised fasting at Buchinger Wilhelmi — rigorously documented across metabolism, longevity, the microbiome, and the body's innate capacity for renewal.

What happens when you fast

Fasting helps your body heal and renew itself from the inside out. It triggers the body's natural cleanup and repair system through autophagy, clearing out damaged cells and recycling them into something useful again.

By extending the time without food, the body uses up its sugar stores, called glycogen. Insulin levels are kept low and stored fat becomes accessible.

Fatty acids are released, and the liver converts part of this fat into ketone bodies, providing a clean and efficient fuel for the brain and muscles — a state known as ketosis.

Once in ketosis, the body shifts into a remarkably stable energy state. Ketones efficiently power the brain and muscles, while preserved muscle protein keeps strength and mobility intact across long fasts.

Hormonal regulation re-balances: growth hormone rises, insulin remains low, and stress responses are dampened by parasympathetic activation — the "rest-and-digest" branch of the nervous system.

In our 1,422-patient study, 93.2% of participants reported improvements in physical and emotional wellbeing during fasting, alongside the absence of hunger.

Mood lifts, sleep deepens, and mental clarity sharpens — supported by stable ketone-fuelled brain energy and a calmer autonomic nervous system.

Refeeding is its own carefully designed phase. The body re-introduces nutrients in a stepwise way that preserves the benefits of fasting and allows tissues to rebuild stronger.

Insulin sensitivity is restored, the gut microbiome reorganises around the new metabolic state, and many of the molecular shifts triggered by fasting persist for weeks or months.

How fasting helps different conditions

Heart & circulation

Cardiovascular

Heart-related risk factors are where supervised fasting shows some of its biggest and most repeatable effects — across thousands of patients, in real clinics, under real medical supervision.

  • 1,610 patients were monitored across 4–41 days of medically supervised fasting — one of the largest prospective datasets ever assembled on this question.
  • Blood pressure fell on average by −6.5 / −3.8 mmHg during the fast — and the drop persisted through the 4 days of food reintroduction.
  • In hypertensive subjects, blood pressure dropped by −24.7 / −13.1 mmHg — a clinically meaningful change for cardiovascular risk.
  • 23.6% of medicated hypertensive patients were able to stop their blood-pressure drugs entirely; another 43.5% reduced their dose — all under medical supervision.
  • In patients with metabolic syndrome, the Framingham 10-year general cardiovascular disease risk score improved in 85% of cases after fasting.
  • Triglycerides, total cholesterol, LDL and very-low-density lipoprotein all fell significantly after 14 days of fasting.
  • HDL ("good cholesterol") rose to levels higher than baseline one month after refeeding, with improved cholesterol-efflux capacity — a functional measure of cardiovascular protection.
  • Fasting raised the Omega-3 Index, a recognised cardiovascular-health marker, and reduced industrial trans fatty acids in red-blood-cell membranes.
  • In the GENESIS sub-study, heart rate variability improved (p < 0.001), pointing to a healthier autonomic balance that persisted after refeeding.

Sources: JAHA 2020 (1,610 patients) · Eur J Nutr 2021 · Atherosclerosis 2024 · Eur J Clin Invest 2025 · Int J Obesity 2025 (GENESIS)

Gut & microbiome

Gastrointestinal

The gut isn't a passive tube — it's a community of trillions of bacteria and viruses that shape immunity, metabolism and inflammation. Fasting reorganises that community in measurable, reproducible ways.

  • Across 92 patients fasting for about 10 days, roughly two-thirds of all bacterial species shifted significantly — 306 went down and 210 went up.
  • Bacteria that feed on dietary fibre fell; bacteria that feed on host-derived molecules rose — a clean biological signature of the body switching its fuel source.
  • 382 of 721 blood metabolites changed significantly in parallel, directly linking the gut shift to whole-body metabolism.
  • In a 2025 gut-virome study of 89 patients, 121 viral species shifted at end-of-fast, and 49 bacteriophages showed reproducible responses across three independent fasting cohorts.
  • Many virome shifts persisted up to 3 months after fasting ended — these are durable, not transient effects.
  • LBP (a blood marker of bacterial-toxin exposure) fell significantly during fasting and stayed low after refeeding — suggesting less inflammatory leakage from the gut.
  • Gut-microbiota function — measured via short-chain-fatty-acid (butyrate) production — was improved 3 months after the fast ended.
  • Fasting helps you keep your teeth longer as you age: we documented anti-inflammatory effects on the gums and a reduction of bacterial pathogens in the mouth, helping treat periodontitis.
  • Periodontitis-linked oral bacteria (e.g. Porphyromonas) decreased measurably during fasting; some oral-microbiome changes persisted 3 months after refeeding.

Sources: bioRxiv 2024 (92 patients) · medRxiv 2025 gut virome (89 patients) · J Nutr Sci 2019 · Quintessence 2024 oral microbiome & periodontitis · Trends Microbiol 2023 (review)

Mood, stress & sleep

Mental health

A common worry is that fasting must feel terrible. The data say the opposite — patients overwhelmingly feel better, and the underlying nervous-system signature confirms it.

  • In the world's largest fasting study (1,422 patients), 93.2% reported the absence of hunger feeling during fasting — once ketosis sets in, appetite drops naturally.
  • Around 84% of patients reported improved psychological well-being after fasting.
  • Among the 404 patients who arrived with a primary complaint, 85% reported an improvement of that complaint through fasting.
  • In the GENESIS sub-study, fasting shifted the nervous system into a relaxation mode — parasympathetic ("rest-and-digest") dominance rose (p < 0.001) while sympathetic activation fell (p < 0.001).
  • This relaxation shift persists after food reintroduction — it is not just a transient effect of being in the fasting state.
  • Heart rate variability (RMSSD) — a recognised marker of resilience and recovery — improved significantly (p < 0.001); sleep quality remained stable.
  • Urinary cortisol — the main stress hormone — fell by −36% by the end of a 10-day fast and stayed low during refeeding.
  • This parasympathetic shift is linked to improved interoception, emotional regulation and mindfulness — fasting feels calming, not depleting.
  • In long-COVID patients, fasting improved cognitive impairment, fatigue and mood — symptoms many patients had carried for months.

Sources: PLOS ONE 2019 (1,422 patients) · Int J Obesity 2025 GENESIS · J Cachexia Sarcopenia Muscle 2021 · Frontiers in Nutrition 2023 (long COVID)

Diabetes, weight & fatty liver

Metabolic disorders

Metabolic syndrome, type-2 diabetes and fatty liver are the defining health challenges of our era. Fasting moves the dial on every one of them — with effects that hold up after refeeding.

  • In 1,422 patients, 84% of pre-existing chronic complaints — type-2 diabetes, fatty liver, high cholesterol, high blood pressure — improved during medically supervised fasting.
  • Blood glucose dropped reliably to the low-normal range; ketone bodies appeared in urine in essentially everyone, confirming the metabolic switch.
  • In 1,610 patients monitored for ketosis, fasting safely induced deep ketosis from day 2; patients with the strongest ketosis had the largest drops in glucose, HbA1c, weight and waist circumference.
  • Repeated long-term fasting sends type-2 diabetes patients into remission — documented in a 57-year-old who achieved sustained weight loss, lower HbA1c, restored insulin sensitivity and sharply reduced drug dependence.
  • About half of prediabetic patients were no longer prediabetic (HbA1c normalised) 3 months after fasting.
  • In 697 patients with elevated Fatty Liver Index, FLI dropped by an average of 14 points (19 in type-2 diabetics); almost half shifted to a lower risk category.
  • Each additional fasting day raised the chance of transforming a manifest fatty liver into a lower risk category by +40%.
  • Visceral fat is eliminated during long-term fasting — and body composition and cardiovascular risks remain improved up to 4 months after refeeding.
  • Even a 5-day at-home modified fast (~600 kcal/day) produced lasting weight loss (−3.1 kg a month later) and durable improvements in cholesterol and LDL.

Sources: PLOS ONE 2019 (1,422 patients) · Nutrients 2024 ketosis (1,610) · Nutrients 2019 FLI (697) · Frontiers Clin Diab 2025 T2DM case report · medRxiv 2024 modified fast · GENESIS follow-up

Muscle, bone & strength

Musculoskeletal

"You'll lose all your muscle" is the most common worry about long fasts. The MRI and strength data — across two independent studies — say the opposite: fasting preserves muscle health, and in healthy individuals muscular performance can even improve.

  • Fasting preserves muscle health: in the GENESIS 12-day fast, body weight fell by −7.4% (5.9 kg) while skeletal muscle volume fell by just −5.4% (542 ml).
  • Most of that "muscle loss" is glycogen depletion (1–2%) and bound water (3–4%) — actual protein loss is likely minimal.
  • Endurance capacity improved: the first ventilatory threshold rose by +10%, meaning daily tasks like walking feel easier.
  • VO₂max (maximum oxygen uptake) was unchanged after 12 days of fasting — aerobic capacity is preserved.
  • Strength is preserved — and even improves in healthy individuals: maximum voluntary contraction rose by +4.6% in older subjects, +2.4% in young subjects and +4.2% in males.
  • In a separate 10-day fast in healthy men, strength in weight-bearing muscles (legs) rose by +33%.
  • Mitochondrial function improved in women — surpassing men, expressing better fat-oxidation capability at day 12.
  • MRI showed no structural or inflammatory changes in muscle — the muscle tissue itself remains healthy.
  • This pattern looks more like the seasonal-fasting adaptation seen in wild animals than the muscle wasting seen in calorie-restricted diets.

Sources: J Cachexia Sarcopenia Muscle 2025 GENESIS (12-day) · J Cachexia Sarcopenia Muscle 2021 (10-day, healthy men)

Chronic low-grade inflammation

Inflammation

Fasting's effect on inflammation has been the most-debated chapter of the field. A large reanalysis settles the puzzle: it depends on where you start.

  • Across 1,422 patients, fasting reduced CRP — but only in patients whose baseline CRP was already elevated.
  • Patients with low baseline CRP often showed a transient rise — explaining decades of contradictory headlines.
  • The pattern is biphasic: a short adaptive stress reaction, followed by longer-term immune recalibration.
  • Chronic inflammation decreases during fasting and through food reintroduction — the anti-inflammatory shift is not undone when patients return to eating.
  • Fasting helps manage rheumatoid arthritis — the founding clinical observation of the Buchinger method, supported a century later by the immune-recalibration data.
  • In long-COVID patients, perceived overall health improved alongside fatigue, breathlessness, muscle and joint pains — symptoms many patients had carried for months.
  • Among 7 measured microRNAs, miR-146-5p — a known anti-inflammatory regulator — showed the strongest fasting-related changes.
  • LBP (a blood marker of bacterial-toxin-driven inflammation) fell during fasting and stayed low after refeeding, suggesting durable reduction of gut-derived inflammatory load.
  • In a 5-day modified fast, blood metabolomics revealed a significant decrease in chronic inflammation markers.
  • Anti-inflammatory effects are real — and most pronounced in the patients who need them the most.

Sources: Front Clin Diab Healthcare 2025 (1,422 patients) · Food Chem Toxicol 2021 microRNAs · J Nutr Sci 2019 LBP · Frontiers in Nutrition 2023 long COVID · medRxiv 2024 modified fast

Brain & nervous system

Neurological

The brain doesn't shut down during fasting — it switches fuels. Ketone bodies are a clean, efficient alternative, and several pathways activated by fasting are at the centre of modern neuroscience research.

  • The metabolic switch from glucose to ketones is documented in 1,422 patients; ketones supply up to 60–70% of brain energy needs during long-term fasting.
  • Long-term fasting deactivates mTOR and activates AMPK — the same molecular pathways implicated in neurodegenerative disease and longevity research.
  • In a long-COVID case series, patients reported improvements in cognitive impairment, smell and taste disorders, and persistent brain fog.
  • Sustained improvements continued even after a return to normal diet — fasting's effects on the brain outlast the fast itself.
  • Long-term fasting remodels fatty-acid composition in red-blood-cell membranes — opening research questions in diseases like multiple sclerosis where myelin (the brain's fatty insulation) is damaged.
  • The parasympathetic activation seen in GENESIS (p < 0.001) is linked to better interoception, emotional regulation and mindfulness.
  • Spermidine — essential for autophagy and neuroprotection in both animals and humans — rose uniformly in fasting patients (Buchinger Wilhelmi cohorts).
  • Fasting-associated microRNAs target cellular pathways including mTOR, FoxO and autophagy — central regulators of brain ageing.

Sources: Frontiers in Nutrition 2023 long COVID · Ann Med 2020 (review) · Eur J Clin Invest 2025 fatty acids · Int J Obesity 2025 GENESIS · Nat Cell Biol 2024 spermidine · Food Chem Toxicol 2021 microRNAs

Immune recalibration

Auto-immune

The clinic itself was founded on auto-immune disease. Otto Buchinger turned to supervised fasting in 1919 to treat his own severe rheumatoid arthritis — the historical seed of the modern programme.

  • 1919: Otto Buchinger recovers from severe rheumatoid arthritis through supervised fasting — the founding clinical observation of the method.
  • Across 404 patients with pre-existing chronic complaints (a subset of the 1,422-patient cohort), 84% reported improvement — including arthritis, type-2 diabetes, fatty liver and hypercholesterolaemia.
  • Adverse effects were reported in < 1% of the 1,422 supervised fasting patients — establishing the safety profile of the method.
  • In long-COVID patients (a model immune-dysregulation condition), perceived overall health improved, and sustained gains persisted after return to normal diet.
  • Fasting activates autophagy — the body's cellular-cleanup pathway, central to immune-cell turnover (Nat Cell Biol 2024).
  • LBP (bacterial-toxin exposure) and chronic low-grade inflammation both fell during fasting, addressing two engines of auto-immune disease.
  • The 5-day modified fast also produced a measurable decrease in chronic inflammation markers — an accessible entry point for outpatient care.
  • In our 1,422-patient cohort, white-blood-cell and platelet counts decreased significantly but remained in the normal range — consistent with controlled immune renewal rather than suppression.

Sources: PLOS ONE 2019 (1,422 patients, 404 with complaints) · Frontiers in Nutrition 2023 long COVID · Nat Cell Biol 2024 spermidine/autophagy · J Nutr Sci 2019 · medRxiv 2024 modified fast

Healthy ageing

Longevity

Fasting activates the same handful of pathways that lengthen lifespan across yeast, flies, mice and humans — and we have the world's longest medically documented repeated-fasting history to look at.

  • A 92-year-old patient fasted 21 days every year for 45 years under medical supervision at Buchinger Wilhelmi — the world's longest documented case of repeated long-term fasting.
  • Exceptional mental and physical health, full mobility, no frailty at 92 — clinical parameters returned reliably to normal after every refeeding cycle.
  • His biological age, measured with epigenetic clocks, was 5.9 years younger than his chronological age — a clear deceleration of multiple ageing clocks.
  • IGF-1 is reduced during long-term fasting — the same growth-signalling pathway whose downregulation extends lifespan in every model organism ever studied.
  • Oxidative stress drops: lipid peroxidation falls and total antioxidant capacity rises across 109 patients after a 10-day fast.
  • Inflammaging improves: the chronic low-grade inflammation that drives age-related disease decreases during fasting and stays low after refeeding (LBP, CRP in elevated-baseline patients, IL markers).
  • Long-term fasting triggers the canonical anti-ageing programme: autophagy, AMPK, SIRT1, and inhibition of mTOR — the same pathways that extend lifespan in every model organism ever tested.
  • Spermidine — essential for fasting-mediated autophagy and lifespan extension across species — rose uniformly in Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting cohorts (Nat Cell Biol 2024). Blocking spermidine synthesis abolishes these effects in animal models, establishing it as causal.
  • Long-term fasting (5–21 days) is safe to repeat throughout the year under medical supervision — and this 45-year case suggests the ageing-deceleration effect compounds over a lifetime.

Sources: JICM 2024 92-year-old case report & epigenetic age · Nat Cell Biol 2024 spermidine · Antioxidants 2020 redox (109 patients) · Food Chem Toxicol 2020 oxidative stress · Ann Med 2020 (review) · PHT 2023 self-healing & longevity

Toxic load & oxidative defence

Detoxification

"Detox" is often dismissed as marketing — but the body really does mobilise stored toxic metals and recalibrate oxidative defences during long-term fasting. We measured it.

  • After a 10-day fast, urinary arsenic excretion fell by −72% and nickel by −15%; hair lead concentrations dropped by −30%.
  • Urinary mercury was unchanged — fasting selectively mobilises some toxic metals more than others, consistent with their biology.
  • Glyphosate (the world's most-used herbicide), chromium and cobalt were undetectable in most participants — useful background data on environmental exposure.
  • Lipid peroxidation (a marker of oxidative damage) fell across 109 patients during a 10-day fast.
  • Total antioxidant capacity in plasma rose, including the ability to scavenge ABTS and hydroxyl radicals — fasting strengthens the body's own oxidative defences.
  • Four weeks after the fast, the share of participants eating mostly organic food rose to 40%, and smoking dropped significantly — fasting acts as a launchpad for healthier habits.
  • Participants also reported improvements in fatigue, sleep, headache and hunger after the fast ended.
  • Across 1,422 patients, 84% of pre-existing chronic complaints improved — consistent with a meaningful reduction in chronic toxic and inflammatory load.

Sources: Frontiers in Nutrition 2021 heavy metals · Antioxidants 2020 redox status (109 patients) · Food Chem Toxicol 2020 baseline-dependent effects · PLOS ONE 2019 (1,422 patients)

Science

A time-proven practice rooted in science and wisdom

A century of medically supervised fasting at Buchinger Wilhelmi — rigorously documented across metabolism, longevity, the microbiome, and the body's innate capacity for renewal.

What happens when you fast

Fasting helps your body heal and renew itself from the inside out. It triggers the body's natural cleanup and repair system through autophagy, clearing out damaged cells and recycling them into something useful again.

By extending the time without food, the body uses up its sugar stores, called glycogen. Insulin levels are kept low and stored fat becomes accessible.

Fatty acids are released, and the liver converts part of this fat into ketone bodies, providing a clean and efficient fuel for the brain and muscles — a state known as ketosis.

Once in ketosis, the body shifts into a remarkably stable energy state. Ketones efficiently power the brain and muscles, while preserved muscle protein keeps strength and mobility intact across long fasts.

Hormonal regulation re-balances: growth hormone rises, insulin remains low, and stress responses are dampened by parasympathetic activation — the "rest-and-digest" branch of the nervous system.

In our 1,422-patient study, 93.2% of participants reported improvements in physical and emotional wellbeing during fasting, alongside the absence of hunger.

Mood lifts, sleep deepens, and mental clarity sharpens — supported by stable ketone-fuelled brain energy and a calmer autonomic nervous system.

Refeeding is its own carefully designed phase. The body re-introduces nutrients in a stepwise way that preserves the benefits of fasting and allows tissues to rebuild stronger.

Insulin sensitivity is restored, the gut microbiome reorganises around the new metabolic state, and many of the molecular shifts triggered by fasting persist for weeks or months.

How fasting helps different conditions

Cardiovascular

Across 1,610 fasting subjects, both systolic and diastolic blood pressure fell consistently and substantially during long-term fasting under medical supervision (JAHA 2020). Heart rate variability — a marker of autonomic balance — improved durably, persisting weeks after refeeding.

In hypertensive patients, antihypertensive medication was reduced or stopped in roughly two thirds of cases, all under medical oversight as clinical parameters improved.

Gastrointestinal

Fasting profoundly reshapes the gut microbiome — the trillions of bacteria, viruses and fungi that regulate immunity, metabolism and inflammation. Bacterial communities shift in step with the metabolic switch from glucose to ketone fuel.

A 2025 preprint extended this picture to the gut virome, showing structured, largely reversible reorganisation of bacteriophage communities, with many shifts persisting for up to three months after refeeding.

Mental health

Fasting does not cause distress — it relieves it. In our 1,422-patient study, 93.2% of participants reported improvements in physical and emotional wellbeing during fasting.

The 2025 GENESIS sub-study showed that fasting shifts the autonomic nervous system into a calmer, more restorative state — better sleep, better mood, lower stress — by activating the parasympathetic branch and measurably improving heart rate variability.

Improved wellbeing93.2%

Metabolic disorders

Fasting blood glucose consistently decreases to low-normal range during Buchinger Wilhelmi fasting, reflecting the shift from glucose to fat and ketone metabolism. Visceral fat — the metabolically harmful fat surrounding abdominal organs — falls by 20.8% (MRI confirmed), with liver fat dropping from 2.6% to 1.4%.

A 2025 case report (Frontiers) documented a clinically reversed type-2 diabetes through repeated long-term fasting: improved HbA1c, restored insulin sensitivity, sharply reduced drug dependence.

Visceral fat−20.8%
Glucose↓13%
LDL↓9%

Musculoskeletal

Fasting does not destroy muscle — this fear is not supported by the evidence. In the GENESIS study (12 days, 250 kcal/day), body weight fell by 7.4% while skeletal muscle volume fell by just 5.4% — a substantially better ratio than seen with calorie-restricted diets.

Muscle strength was maintained and improved by up to 33% in the best responders. At the cellular level, fasting activates mitochondrial biogenesis, boosting the energy-producing capacity of muscle cells.

Strength gain+33%

Inflammation

Our 2025 Frontiers in Nutrition paper revisited the anti-inflammatory effects of long-term fasting and found that baseline CRP status determines the magnitude of response: patients with high inflammation see steep, durable reductions, while already-low-CRP patients show little change.

This reframes fasting as a baseline-dependent therapy and informs clinical patient selection — anti-inflammatory effects are real, but most pronounced where they are most needed.

MechanismBaseline-dependent

Neurological

Ketone bodies provide a clean and efficient fuel for the brain, with neuroprotective effects observed across multiple lines of research. A 2023 case series and literature review examined long-term fasting in five long-COVID patients and reported improvements in cognitive impairment, fatigue, and disorders of smell and taste.

The proposed mechanisms include anti-inflammatory action, autophagy induction in neurons, immune recalibration and gut-brain microbiome remodelling.

Mechanisms4 pathways

Auto-immune

Otto Buchinger's clinic was founded in 1919 after he himself recovered from severe rheumatoid arthritis through supervised fasting — an origin story that grounds the modern clinical programme.

Across our patient cohort, 84% of pre-existing chronic complaints — including arthritis, fatty liver, hypertension, hypercholesterolaemia and type-2 diabetes — improved during medically supervised fasting, supported by autophagy, immune-cell turnover and reduced systemic inflammation.
